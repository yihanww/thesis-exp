Purpose of the Study:

This study examines to what extent people agree upon impressions of unfamiliar faces. You were tasked to evaluate each face on one attribute, yet other participants may have been asked to use a different attribute. While previous studies acknowledge our ability to collectively agree upon certain traits regarding certain faces, this study questions how differences exist. For example, you may rate a particular face a 1 on a 1 to 7 scale, and when you’re asked to re-evaluate the same face, you may rate it a 2. Collectively, many people might agree that it’s about a 2.15. So what does this variation mean?

Here are a couple possibilities: 1) the inconsistency is simply due to errors—in other words, our impressions are imprecise & consequently so are the evaluations we make. 2) Every person has a similar, but genuinely different impression. This study will be informative about the individual differences in psychological research.

As this study is ongoing, we ask that you not discuss this study with anyone else who is currently participating or might participate at a future time.
