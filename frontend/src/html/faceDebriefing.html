Trait Judgments of Faces

This project examines what aspects of faces are important when developing first impressions of others. For example, what kind of faces do people typically have in mind when they think about different groups of people in society? By looking at people’s judgment on various faces (e.g., which face looks more like a certain group or identity) as well as people’s ratings on these faces on a trait (e.g., how attractive or trustworthy they look), we can extract what facial features affect people’s preconceptions about individuals and social groups and how those preconceptions give rise to specific trait judgments.
